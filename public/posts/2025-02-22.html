Good god, man

2025 February 22

I drove a friend home from a party last night. Our conversation turned to the Recent Horrors and how they affect us. The sinking feeling in our guts after every news update timidly reporting the elimination of transgender people from public life. Friends displaced, documents shredded, medical care lost, jobs erased, lives torn apart. Budgeting to afford a sanctuary city -- what even is a sanctuary city? Transphobic carnage as televised sport, monetized, brought to you by Elon, by Dogecoin‚Ñ¢. Sam Nordquist. Good god, man. He should be alive and well.

One thing my friend said stuck out to me: her cis friends (at least the ones who even know what's going on) feel sad about what's happening, while she and her trans friends feel scared.

Yeah. I'm scared too. I wasn't sure if I would respond to our current circumstances with fear -- all trans people experience systemic/personal abandonment in some way, the multiply marginalized among us particularly so. We often develop emotional calluses that allow us to power through it, even if we shouldn't have to. I've tried to ignore the fear and to focus on emotions that motivate action more easily -- devastation, anger, pain.

But good god man, I'm scared. And I'm tired too -- tired from all the planning, paperwork, phone calls with my doctors, phone calls with estranged family, deciding where to run if shit goes down around me. Haven't I run enough already? Why do I have to run in the first place?

My friend and I agreed on how important it is to lean on friends and community right now, to look out for one another as what little systemic "support" we ever had falls apart. I'm sure I'll feel better in good company, I truly believe that. I just wish I didn't have to be afraid at all.
