Hello there! Ann Arbor experienced another round of beautifully warm weather this past week. Multiple days in the low 70s, lots of sunshine... it was the best. Now we've returned to another round of cold and wind... I wonder how the outdoors will feel once spring officially starts next week? 

### PhD & Research
Now that I'm through the UMSI pre-candidacy milestone, now it is time for me to prepare for... another milestone! The next milestone following our pre-candidacy defense is our field preliminary milestone -- essentially an extended literature review where we identify a specialized research focus, synthesize existing literature within that focus, and identifying ways to move that research focus forward. I'll primarily work on my prelims throughout late spring and summer this year... I wonder how the end result will turn out? :)

In other news: happy to announce that I will be attending the <a href="https://sites.google.com/view/between-and-beyond/home-page?authuser=0" target="blank"><b>Between and Beyond: Designing for Identity Complexity in HCI</b></a> workshop at CHI 2026 this year! Excited to discuss the friction between categorical framings in HCI and the fluid/context-specific/contested nature of identity, the implications of this friction for people whose experiences/identities do not fit cleanly within these categories, and how HCI systems could better support <i>"identity as relational and evolving, rather than as fixed categories."</i> :) I wound up writing a short comic for this workshop about the difficulty of depicting identity complexity on social media platforms that impose such rigid identity categories on their users. Will post the comic to my website after a few typesetting edits! 

Also very excited to attend CHI 2026 generally -- this will be my first time ever visiting Europe! I've traveled so much more since starting the PhD than I ever have before... deeply grateful for the opportunity, it is wonderful feeling my world grow :) 

### Life & Birds 

One item I bought from Meijer last weekend was an 8 lb bag of unsalted, in-shell peanuts for the Blue Jays who visit my balcony bird feeders. The Blue Jays eat more peanuts lately now that the weather's warming up; the 4 lb bag I bought last time only lasted a month and a a half, and I'm not sure the 8 lb bag will last much longer! These corvids are eating me out of house and home... but here I am, feeding them all the same.
